# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Toolkitty a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience, ability, or viewpoint.

## Expected behavior

- Be considerate, patient, and specific in technical discussion.
- Critique ideas and changes, not people.
- Assume good intent while remaining open to correction.
- Respect privacy, consent, and project security boundaries.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope and help keep discussions on topic.
- Acknowledge mistakes, apologize when appropriate, and repair harm where possible.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or attention, or sharing sexual or violent material where it is not clearly relevant.
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, deliberate disruption, or sustained bad-faith argument.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Pressuring someone to disclose identity, personal circumstances, credentials, or private project data.
- Exploiting security reports, community access, or maintainer privileges to harm others.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies in the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, reviews, and other spaces where someone represents Toolkitty.

Report conduct concerns privately to [@0cwa](https://github.com/0cwa) through GitHub. Do not start a public confrontation or include sensitive personal information in an issue. The maintainer will review reports as impartially and confidentially as practical and may remove content, issue a warning, limit participation, temporarily suspend access, or permanently ban a participant depending on severity and pattern.

Anyone enforcing this policy must disclose conflicts of interest and protect the privacy and safety of reporters and affected people. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an investigation is itself a violation.

This policy is informed by the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct.html).
